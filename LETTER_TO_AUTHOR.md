**Subject:** IDLE_DIRECTIVE — community body-grid planner (tribute)

Hi kevinfu510,

First of all — thank you for the IDLE_DIRECTIVE **demo**. I picked it
up recently and have been enjoying it a lot, and I'm already looking
forward to the full release on 29 July 2026. The body grid puzzle
(where every Spinner needs its Repeater adjacency, batteries fight for
bus access, and you only ever have just-not-enough room) is what hooked
me, and what gave me the idea below.

You're **the first person I'm reaching out to** about this — I wanted
to check with you before any wider announcement, since the project
borrows so much from your demo.

Once I had ~20+ components in the demo I started hand-arranging the
body for too long per session. So I wrote a little web tool that
mirrors the body grid and runs a Simulated Annealing search to find
good layouts — it preserves the component names, shapes and
terminology from the demo so the result drops back into the actual
body 1:1. (I'll happily extend it once the full release lands, if any
new components or rules need supporting.)

A few things up front:

- **It's an unofficial fan project, built as a tribute and a thank-you
  to you.** No monetisation, no ads, no in-game value extraction — just
  a planner. The interface design and visuals are my own; the
  **component shapes, names, and terminology come from your game**.
- The Help modal and Settings → About panel both **link back to your
  Steam and itch.io pages** and credit you by name.
- Source: <https://github.com/Zdendys79/idle-directive>
- Live build: <https://idle-directive.zdendys79.website>

**If you'd prefer it taken down, renamed, or restricted in any way**,
please just say so and I'll act on it — no questions asked, it's your
IP. Equally, if you'd like to collaborate, take it over, or have me add
a particular disclaimer / link / change, I'm happy to do that.

Either way — congratulations on the game and thanks again. Hope to see
it grow.

Best,
Zdendys79
